About Me

I am an incoming PhD student at Boston University, advised by Dr. Josh Peterson, where I'll be using complex machine learning methods to further our understanding of human behavior and clinical applications of these techniques. Outside of research, I can most often be found at the gym, traveling the world with my partner in search of great food and interesting beers, or playing video games a bit too competitively.

Research

I am interested in the intersection of artificial intelligence and psychological/medical research. More specifically, the application of methodologies such as deep learning and generative AI. I plan to implement and improve upon these methodologies to develop better software to increase translational value and bridge theoretical insights through machine learning.

My research is deeply rooted in understanding human decision-making processes and behavioral patterns, exploring how these insights can enhance the development and application of AI systems in healthcare and psychological research.
